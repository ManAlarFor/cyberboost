Origins

The very first Cyberpunk media even before the creation of the term were the american comic Judge Dredd and the japanese manga Akira, both showing a dystopic future and a society colapse. However, the start of this genre is almost always settled by some writters that wrote stories fulled of social critics, as William Gibson, with his book Neuromancer, as well as other authors like Bruce Sterling and John Shirley.

The retrofuturism is a mix of different literature and art movements, which are based on the image that people had in the past about how the future was going to be. Cyberpunk is one of this flows, as well as other as Steampunk, based of the Industrial Revolution; Dieselpunk, based on the technology used on the Cold War; Atompunk based on the Atomic power and usualy settled in post-apocaliptic world; or Solarpunk, where the solar power is fully used.
